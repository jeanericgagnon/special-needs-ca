# State Complexity Scorecard

This document classifies every remaining US state by upgrade complexity (Easy, Moderate, Complex, or Very Complex) based on population density, county counts, and the structures of their developmental and educational routing systems.

---

## 1. Complexity Tier Criteria

*   **Easy:** Centralized, state-administered systems. Low county counts (e.g. < 15) or single-district structures. Zero overlapping catchments. Highly structured official directories.
*   **Moderate:** Clean county-based administration. Simple 1:1 mapping between counties and offices. No overlapping catchments or metropolitan borough splits.
*   **Complex:** Decentralized regional catchments (e.g. multi-county boards, regional entities). High county counts (e.g. 50–100). High volume of local school districts requiring reference audits.
*   **Very Complex:** Overlapping regional catchments (many-to-many county-agency relationships), major metropolitan splits (e.g. NYC borough offices), or highly fragmented local authority systems (like California's Regional Centers or Texas LIDDAs).
