## 2. Gap Breakdown for High-Priority States

### 🤠 Texas
- **Coverage:** Excellent structural coverage across all 254 counties.
- **Verification Gaps:** All records are classified as `source_listed`. While they are verified against official state rosters (such as the HHSC ECI and LIDDA rosters), they lack human/VA verification of localized direct lines.

### 🌴 Florida
- **Coverage:** APD Area offices, ESE regional districts, and Early Steps catchments are fully mapped.
- **Verification Gaps:** Only 1 school district remains in `manual_review_required` status. 52 school districts are marked `source_supported_ready_to_stage` and are ready for promotion.

### 🔔 Pennsylvania
- **Coverage:** Intermediate Units (IUs) and MH/ID local intakes are fully source-listed.
- **Verification Gaps:** Lacks deep nonprofit coverage (only 8 records exist in the database, all marked `pending_review`). Needs nonprofit seeding.

### 🍎 New York
- **Coverage:** Mapped across all 62 counties (OPWDD regional developmental offices and LDSS offices).
- **Verification Gaps:** 40 out of 62 school districts remain in manual review due to missing special education contacts.

### 🌰 Ohio
- **Coverage:** County Board of Developmental Disabilities (CBDD) offices and CDJFS local benefits offices are fully mapped.
- **Verification Gaps:** 166 out of 176 school districts are blocked in manual review due to lack of verified direct contacts.

### 🍑 Georgia
- **Coverage:** Mapped structurally, but blocked due to a high manual review rate.
- **Verification Gaps:** 132 county offices and 132 school districts are blocked in manual review, representing a massive recovery bottleneck.
