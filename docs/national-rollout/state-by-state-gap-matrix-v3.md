## 2. Core Gap Dimension Definitions

1. **School District Special Education Contacts:**
   - **Gap:** Most states have empty placeholder slots or statewide general phone numbers listed under local school district pages.
   - **Rule:** Only direct special education departments or program administrator phone numbers/emails qualify.
   - **Remediation:** Deferred to the human manual review queue.

2. **County HHS/DSS Local Intake Routing:**
   - **Gap:** Multiple states point to generic statewide headquarters instead of county-level offices where eligibility determinations actually take place.
   - **Rule:** County-specific offices (with verified physical locations) are required.

3. **DD/IDD and Early Intervention Routing Confidence:**
   - **High:** Complete mapping of catchment areas or local authorities (e.g., Texas LIDDA, Florida APD, Pennsylvania MH/ID).
   - **Medium:** Partial catchments mapped, or regional offices configured but missing localized intake hours/direct extensions.
   - **Low:** Generic statewide links only.

4. **Nonprofit Depth & Clinic Depth:**
   - **Gap:** High-priority states like Pennsylvania and New York are structurally complete but have very few verified local support nonprofits seeded in the database.
   - **Remediation:** Direct seeding from trusted directories during the next 30 days.

## 3. Score Honesty Audit Results

All states have had their depth scores adjusted downwards to reflect the exclusion of directory-routed listings and manual review queues. No state is allowed to artificially inflate its score by labeling generic hotlines or unverified commercial providers as local "verified" resources.
