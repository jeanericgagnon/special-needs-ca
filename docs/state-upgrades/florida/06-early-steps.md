# Florida Early Steps / Part C early intervention

Analysis of Florida Part C early intervention routing.

## 1. Florida Early Steps Structure
*   **Statewide Program:** Florida Early Steps is the state's early intervention program under Part C of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).
*   **Lead Agency:** The Florida Department of Health (DOH) Children's Medical Services (CMS) is the designated lead agency.
*   **Local Programs:** Operations are decentralized across **15 Local Early Steps programs** hosted by local community organizations (universities, Easterseals, hospitals, health planning councils).
*   **Referral/Intake Route:** Anyone can refer a child (parents, doctors, caregivers) by calling the local office or using the statewide toll-free number (1-800-218-0001). Referrals are routed to the local program office serving the child's residence.
*   **Eligibility Route:** Infants and toddlers (ages 0 to 3) with a diagnosed physical or mental condition likely to result in developmental delay, or with a developmental delay of at least 25% in one or more developmental areas (cognitive, physical, communication, social/emotional, adaptive).
*   **Transition at Age 3:** Transition planning starts at least 90 days before the child's 3rd birthday to move to Part B services under local school districts and FDLRS.
*   **Complaint/Dispute Process:** Administrative complaints, mediation, or due process hearings filed through Children's Medical Services (DOH).
*   **Official Source URLs:**
    *   State Program Portal: https://www.floridahealth.gov/programs-and-services/childrens-medical-services/early-steps/index.html
    *   Early Steps Directory: https://www.floridaearlysteps.com

## 2. Local Program & Service Area Mapping
The 15 regional programs serve all 67 counties. Mappings are regional and host-hosted, with Miami-Dade County split between two portals:
1.  **Western Panhandle Early Steps** (Ascension Sacred Heart): Escambia, Santa Rosa, Okaloosa, Walton.
2.  **Big Bend Early Steps** (Children's Home Society): Leon, Gadsden, Jefferson, Madison, Taylor, Wakulla, Franklin, Liberty, Bay, Calhoun, Gulf, Holmes, Jackson, Washington.
3.  **Northeastern Early Steps** (UF College of Medicine): Baker, Bradford, Clay, Duval, Nassau, St. Johns.
4.  **North Central Early Steps** (University of Florida): Alachua, Columbia, Dixie, Gilchrist, Hamilton, Lafayette, Levy, Marion, Suwannee, Union.
5.  **North Beaches Early Steps** (Easterseals): Flagler, Lake, Putnam, Sumter, Volusia.
6.  **Central Florida Early Steps** (Orlando Health): Orange, Osceola, Seminole.
7.  **Bay Area Early Steps** (University of South Florida): Hillsborough, Polk.
8.  **West Central Early Steps** (Johns Hopkins All Children's): Citrus, Hernando, Pasco, Pinellas.
9.  **Gulf Central Early Steps** (Health Planning Council): Charlotte, DeSoto, Hardee, Highlands, Manatee, Sarasota.
10. **Southwest Florida Early Steps** (Health Planning Council): Collier, Lee, Hendry, Glades.
11. **Gold Coast Early Steps** (Children's Diagnostic & Treatment Center): Broward.
12. **North Dade Early Steps** (University of Miami): Miami-Dade (North).
13. **Southernmost Coast Early Steps** (Easterseals): Miami-Dade (South), Monroe.
14. **Space Coast Early Steps** (Catch of Brevard): Brevard.
15. **Treasure Coast Early Steps** (Easterseals): Palm Beach, Martin, St. Lucie, Indian River, Okeechobee.
